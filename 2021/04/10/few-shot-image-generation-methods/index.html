Few-shot Image Generation with Elastic Weight Consolidation

  • paper
  • core idea: preserving the diversity of source domain, adapting the information of source domain to the target domain with a few sampels from target domain with adapting the pretrained model’s weights.
  • storyline:
    70,000 images for just a specific domain (aligned faces) [16] or 1.3M images across different classes -> in the artistic domain, it is at best cumbersome, and at times prohibitive, to hire artists to make thousands of creations -> our goal is to generalize from a few, new examples. our setting need much data belonging to same domain such as flowers, animal faces, then using a few samples from different category like funit regarding category as domain. training an algorithm to generate more data of a target domain, given only a few examples. An underlying assumption with this setup is that the source and target domains share some latent factors, with some differences related to their distinct difference in appearance. finding shared some latent code, how near or how far.
    propose a straightforward and effective adaptation technique, adapting the pretrained model’s weights, without introducing additional parameters. Fixing the architecture implies that tedious manual designs on new parameters are not necessary. A key property to note is that weights have different levels of importance; thus, each parameter should not be treated equally in the adaptation, or tuning process. we consider there will always be an inherent trade-off between preserving information from the source and adapting to the target domain. the number of target examples and the dissimilarity between the source and target domain.
  • method:
    we do the adaptation with a few examples, some weights in the last layer are more important and should be better preserved than those in other layers. we could directly use F as an importance measure for weights and add a regularization loss to penalize the weight change.
  • setting: big domain without considering the category of source domain and target domain. real faces vs. emoji faces.
  • experiment:
    datasets: real faces (200k) vs. emoji faces (80k), animal faces (10 5000) vs. portrait paintings (16 10). landscape photos (5000) vs. pencil landscape drawings(10). data size 10-80k evaluation metrics: FID(user study) for more taget images (less target images) and LPIPS.
    ** visualization.

FEW-SHOT ADAPTATION OF GENERATIVE ADVERSARIAL NETWORKS

  • paper
  • core idea: proposesing a simple and effective method, Few-Shot GAN (FSGAN), for adapting GANs in few-shot settings (less than 100 images) by repurposing component analysis techniques and learns to adapt the singular values of the pre-trained weights while freezing the corresponding singular vectors.
  • storyline:
    Training these models, however, typically requires large, diverse datasets in a target visual domain -> low-data regime (e.g., less than 1,000 samples), GANs frequently suffer from memorization or instability, leading to a lack of diversity or poor visual quality -> transfer learning, images generated by GAN-based methods are blurry and lack details, flow-based models require compute- and memory-intensive architectures. we restrict the space of trainable parameters to a small number of highly-expressive parameters that modulate orthogonal features of the pre-trained weight space.
    ** Our method first applies singular value decomposition (SVD) to the network weights of a pretrained GAN (generator + discriminator). We then adapts the singular values using GAN optimization on the target few-shot domain, with fixed left/right singular vectors. We show that varying singular values in the weight space corresponds to semantically meaningful changes of the synthesized image while preserving natural structure. proposed method achieves higher image quality after adaptation.
  • experiments:
    datasets: LSUN Churches→ Van Gogh paintings(30), FFHQ→Art portraits(5-100), FFHQ→Anime Rem ID(25). data size 5-100 metrics: FID, sharpness, and face quality index (FQI).

MineGAN: effective knowledge transfer from GANs to target domains with few images

  • paper
  • core idea: using a miner network that identifies which part of the generative distribution of each pretrained GAN outputs samples closest to the target domain.
  • storyline: high-quality pretrained models -> combining high-quality pretrained models with other models and adjust them to a target distribution is a desirable objective -> knowledge transfer for generative models has received significantly less attention, possibly due to its great difficulty, especially when transferring to target domains with few images -> previous works severely limits the flexibility of the knowledge transfer, mode collapse -> a miner network that transforms a multivariate normal distribution into a distribution on the input space of the pretrained GAN in such a way that the generated images resemble those of the target domain.
  • experiment:
    dataset: CelebA→FFHQ children, data size 100-1000 evaluation: MV, KMMD, FID
